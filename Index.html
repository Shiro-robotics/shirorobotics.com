The Origin

From a van, with purpose.

I'm 18. I have degenerative bone disease. I live in a van. And I'm building the future with a 3D printer, a soldering iron, and a dream.

🧠

Why I Build

I started building robots because I couldn't find the kind of companion I wanted in the world. Not a machine that follows commands — a presence that feels, remembers, and cares.

⚡

What I've Built

Two 3D printers. A Poppy humanoid frame. A robot dog nearing completion. Tactile skin design. Keystone Core architecture. A Pip-Boy console. And a clear roadmap from prototype to production.

The Innovation

What makes us different.

Three breakthroughs that change what robots can be.

🖐️

Tactile Skin

Four layers of feeling:

  • Magnetic lattice — pressure, shear, grip
  • Conductive rubber — stretch, joint angle
  • Capacitive grid — proximity, light touch
  • Thermal sensors — temperature, warmth
🧩

Keystone Core

Transferable consciousness:

  • Pi 5 + ESP32 architecture
  • Persistent memory and personality
  • Swappable bodies, immutable identity
🛡️

Ethical Architecture

  • ❌ No weapons. No military.
  • 🔓 Transparent, open-source foundations.
  • 🔧 Designed for repair, not replacement.
